Logo concepts

Four directions for Overdrive.

Each rendered in the existing brand system — dark surface, orange accent, sharp edges, IBM Plex. Shown at favicon (32px), small (48px), and header-scale (large).

01

Clipped waveform

audio overdrive · oscilloscope

A signal pushed past its rails — two cycles of square-like waveform that read as an overdriven / clipped signal. Reference is audio, not automotive — the register that survives in a procurement room. Only engineers will catch it; that's the point.
Strengths
Technical, distinct
No tech-naming collision
Reads at 16px
Kernel-adjacent feel
Tradeoffs
Less immediately "orchestration"
Can look generic if too soft
Benefits from the clipped-rail variant over pure sine

The "O" of Overdrive as a tachometer past the redline — needle at 1 o'clock, tick marker at the top showing the limit being crossed. Most literal interpretation of the name, but stays abstract enough to avoid cheap racing-car clichés.

Recognisable from glance

Risk of "consumer brand" feel
Dense at favicon size
Hardest to render cleanly
at very small scale

Recommendation

01 (waveform) or 02 (current wedge).

01 is the strongest fresh design — most on-brief for the name, distinct in the space, scales cleanly, no collision. 02 is what you already have and has the virtue of being real and working. 03 is the most literal but carries the most visual risk of reading as consumer-brand. 04 is the safest but gives up the favicon.

If you want to lock in a direction: pick 01, keep 02 as backup. If you're not ready to move: 02 is already solid.
